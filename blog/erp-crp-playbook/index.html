Oracle ERP CRP Playbook

A field guide to effective Conference Room Pilots and fit‑gap sessions.

A successful Conference Room Pilot (CRP) can make or break your Oracle ERP implementation. CRP sessions bring together stakeholders to validate configured processes using real‑world scenarios. Here’s my playbook for running effective CRPs and capturing fit‑gap findings.

Define the scope and success criteria

Before the session, agree on which processes will be validated and what constitutes success. Clear scope prevents scope creep and helps focus participants on critical flows.

Prepare data and scripts

Build realistic test scenarios and prepare master and transactional data ahead of time. Good data ensures participants see accurate results and reduces downtime during the session.

Capture and triage issues

Document each issue with an owner, severity and proposed resolution. Prioritize gaps for remediation. Tools like Panaya can streamline logging and tracking.

Communicate decisions

After the session, summarise outcomes and next steps. Stakeholders should understand which gaps will be addressed immediately and which will be deferred. Maintain momentum by scheduling follow‑up sessions.
